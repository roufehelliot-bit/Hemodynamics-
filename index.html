2. Core Hemodynamic Concepts

Preload

Think of preload as how full the ventricle is at the end of diastole. Clinically, we approximate this with EDV/EDP, wedge, or right atrial pressure.

  • ↑ Preload → ↑ stroke volume (up to a point).
  • Flattened Frank–Starling curve in systolic dysfunction.
Afterload

Afterload is the resistance the ventricle ejects against, approximated by systemic vascular resistance (SVR) on the left and pulmonary vascular resistance (PVR) on the right.

  • ↑ Afterload → ↓ stroke volume (if contractility fixed).
  • Clinically: hypertension, vasopressors, aortic stenosis.
Contractility

Intrinsic squeeze of the myocardium, independent of preload and afterload. In practice: inotropes (dobutamine, milrinone), ischemia, cardiomyopathy.

  • ↑ Contractility → ↑ stroke volume at a given preload.
  • PV loop ESPVR slope becomes steeper.

Helpful mental formulas

  • CO = HR × SV
  • MAP ≈ CO × SVR
  • SV ≈ EDV − ESV
